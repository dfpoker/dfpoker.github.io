Class Action Suit filed against Ferguson and Lederer

Posted on April 13, 2012 by Diamond Flush

Last June, online poker players Todd Terry, Nick Hammer, Robin Hougdahl and Steve Segal filed a class action lawsuit  in the Southern District of New York, alleging RICO violations against the Full Tilt companies and various individuals, some of which were Full Tilt shareholders. There were several motions by both the defendant companies and the individuals to dismiss the charges based on lack of jurisdiction and failure to state a claim, and in January 2012 Judge Sand did indeed dismiss  the claims against the individual defendants 1  for lack of jurisdiction. The Court held some of the conversion charges open against only the defendant companies PocketKings, Vantage and Filco, dismissing without prejudice against Tiltware and PocketKings Consulting. All RICO charges were also dismissed at that time for lack of standing.          

Now, the same plaintiffs have filed another class action lawsuit against two principal shareholders and Board of Director members of the Full Tilt Poker companies, Howard Lederer and Chris Ferguson. The new complaint, filed in Nevada on behalf of U.S.A. players, is for Conversion and alleges the defendants exercised “unlawful dominion and control” over the players funds and have interfered with players’ rights to their property by denying them access to their funds. The suit alleges that Ferguson and Lederer, by way of Full Tilt Poker, have conceded that the money is owed to the players by virtue of the continuous statements following Black Friday saying so, for example:

“Please be assured that your funds are safe and we thank you for your patience while we do everything in our power to have your money returned to you as soon as possible.”

Further, the complaint asserts that Lederer and Ferguson, unbeknownst to the FTP players, “executed a massive fraud” by using fake web sites and sham internet merchants deceiving players, banks, credit card and financial institutions, which allowed them to “inequitably acquire” millions of dollars of illicit profits and funds from the player accounts.

The suit alleges figures that have been asserted in the amended civil complaint filed in the Southern District of New York by the USAO, namely that Lederer was paid more than $ 42 million and Ferguson more than $ 85 million in distributions and profit sharing since 2004 as well as having authorized a total of $ 443 million in total distributions over that time period to all shareholders. The complaint also points to millions of dollars in loans that the defendants approved to themselves and other FTP shareholders which were never  repaid. Such loans, like the distributions, were taken from commingled funds which rightfully belonged to the player accounts according to the complaint.

The suit alleges that the defendants are liable for conversion of Plantiff’s and class members moneys that were held in the player accounts and which are currently and wrongfully in the exclusive control of the defendants.  The plaintiff’s ask for the return of the player’s (class) account funds,approximately $ 150 million, interest, penalties and costs.

  1. Claims were dismissed against J. Juanda, Howard Lederer, Chris Ferguson, Jennifer Harmon Traniello, Erick Lindgren, Eric Seidel, Andy Bloch, Mike Matasow, Allan Cunningham and Phil Ivey. ↩
