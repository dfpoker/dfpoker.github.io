Daniel Tzvetkoff spotted in New York City

Posted on April 22, 2012 by Diamond Flush

The Australian daily newspaper, The Courier-Mail, reports today that they spotted former internet and payment processor whiz kid Daniel Tzvetkoff  strolling with his family in New York’s Chinatown district yesterday.

Tzvetkoff, the founder of Intabill, the major payment processor used by each of the major online poker companies to process transactions to and from U.S. customers for several years, was arrested in Las Vegas in 2010.  Shortly thereafter, New York federal authorities had him transferred to New York, and the issue of bail, which was originally granted by a Las Vegas Magistrate Judge, was revisited and the order stayed, based on concerns that Tzvetkoff would not appear to face his charges.       

After that time, each docket entry in the court case regarding Tzvetkoff has been sealed. It is widely believed that he was the driving force behind providing the needed evidence leading up to what is now know as the Black Friday criminal and civil online poker cases, by striking a deal with authorities to become their key informant. It’s also been accepted as true, that he has been in a secret location, under FBI watchdogs, offering his assistance as an informant against his alleged previous partners at the poker companies and other payment processors.

When motions were recently filed in the criminal case against two of the defendants headed for trial earlier this month, Chad Elie and John Campos, information came to light that at least 90,000 documents were provided by Tzvetkoff to be used against his alleged co-conspirators. He never had to appear for testimony however, since both Elie and Campos settled their cases with the U.S. attorneys office prior to the start of the trial.

According to the Courier-Mail report, Tzvetkoff, looking not much different than he had before,  first denied his identity when confronted, then threatened that there was going to be trouble after having been identified.  The article quotes him as saying “Look, you’re going to get in a lot of trouble. There are people with us who you’re going to be in trouble with”.

Although there is no indication that anyone with him or following him, ever approached the reporter, Tzvetkoff gathered his family and made a getaway in a nearby taxi.

This is likely the first in-person spotting of Tzvetkoff since he was placed under protection of authorities pending his expected testimony. Details of his plea agreement, in return for such testimony, is not known.
