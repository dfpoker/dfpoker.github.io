In my case, the loyalty is exclusive to Full Tilt.
I can’t speak for other players, but in my own case, the renewed loyalty was a result of;
– investigating the events which led to the demise to start with, and realizing the placement of guilt was 100% disconnected from the software
– researching and understanding the competence level of PokerStars business management / history
– continuing the well-researched opinion that the games, structures and competition at FTP are second-to-none in the industry

As for why PokerStars themselves made the decision to keep the name – I don’t know. It’s possible their reasons are similar, but it would have been quite the gamble using the same perspective, as they’d have to assume everyone acted as I have throughout the whole mess.
Thus far, I’d say the FTP rebuild is at about 35-45% of what it was pre-BF (not including U.S. player involvement).

I understand why Pokerstars chose to buy Full Tilt … they had a huge client base, which will become loyal to Pokerstars through repayment, and the software they used was, reportedly, some of the best in the business.

But software can be re-branded and re-skinned, and users can be re-directed to other places for actual play. I can’t work out what makes the Full Tilt brand SO valuable that people are willing to ignore the taint that is attached to the name. I understand why Pokerstars bought Full Tilt … I can’t figure out why they are keeping the brand however, instead of re-skinning the software and re-directing the users.
