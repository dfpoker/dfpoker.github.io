Enron’s outside auditing company Arthur Anderson also went bankrupt as a result. If a huge auditing company like Arthur Anderson couldn’t uncover the true financial picture of Enron it wouldn’t shock me that the guys running FT couldn’t pull a fast one on the poker players that owned FT and made up the board. I’m sure as long as the guys actually running FT (Bitar, and the financial weenies) paid out distributions the owners probably trusted the fake financial reports they were shown.

The law passed after Enron went bankrupt, the Sarbanes-Oxley Act to be specific required outside auditing for public companies. FT wasn’t a public company and possibly not audited by anybody that couldn’t be bribed to keep the truth from getting out. You would like to think that if Ray Bitar let the owners know the true state of the companies finances they would have accepted smaller distributions. Problem is they had so many poker playing owners that it wouldn’t take long for one or to to tell a friend. Rumors spread fast in the poker community and the customers would find out. Nobody would deposit if they knew FT didn’t have the money to pay out existing accounts, so Bitar chose to hide the truth as long as he could probably hoping that future profits would bring the company back to financial health.
