Introduction

Posted on February 4, 2012 by Diamond Flush

As many of you know, the founders of Subject: Poker have decided to lock up the site that has been bringing you unbiased, factual poker news since shortly after Black Friday. Countless hours went into research, investigations, and validations of information over the last nine months by the founders of the site and the team that worked with them.  For all of their dedication, they deserve kudos, respect and thanks for all the tireless work that has gone into bringing news to the poker community.

As a previous team member of Subject: Poker, and with a humble heart, I have agreed to launch a site that will continue reporting the news. I am just a casual poker player that has an interest in the facts surrounding the current events, and that’s what I try to share. At no time in the past has this ever been a full time paying job for any of us. It was purely, as was said before, simply a labor of love with the aim to dispel rumors and give up-to-date factual information of news in the poker world. I plan to continue to abide by Subject: Poker’s promise to not accept advertising from the companies I may be writing about, for as long as I can. It is my hope to be able to survive on donations and keep this site afloat, at least until the events leading up to and surrounding Black Friday and it’s aftermath have played out.

The original Subject: Poker site will remain online for readers to view any previous articles, and an archive link is provided near the top of my home page. You will surely see links here referencing those articles. This new site will hopefully provide a similar navigation experience.

I hope to be able to continue working with the invaluable sources of information that have been so helpful in the past. Of course, anyone that believes they have information that could be helpful in reporting facts are encouraged to email me @ diamondflushpoker@gmail.com. Sites such as this one would not be able to provide in-depth factual information without such sources. As always, source identities are protected and can remain confidential.

I look forward to continuing to bring you the news.
